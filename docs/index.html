Sustainability

Hard facts:
- Animal agriculture is the leading cause of climate change

- The industry occupies a third of all arable land (If everyone adapted a vegan diet by 2050, we could sequester 772 GtCO2 which is the equivalent of 21 years of our current fossil fuel emissions)

- The average person only gets 18% of calories from animal products

- Water usage: A vegan uses on average a million liters less water than an omnivore per year. That is an equivolant to 30 years of 10 minute long daily showers.

- Methane has an instantenous GWP 124 times greater than that of CO2.

- Oceans store heat so immediate heating matters.

- Coral reefs are vital for carbon sequestration, but they are dying due to overfishing and increased warmth. At a certain temperature the corals expel the algae (bleaching), they are in a symbiosis with and if it stays too warm they can't let the algae back and the reef dies.

- Honey bees are in direct competition for food with wild bees, which leads to a decrease of 48% of wild bee abundance around bee hive presence. Wild bees are much better pollinators than honey bees.

    • Cognitive Dissonance (A conflict of inconsistency)

    Our brains have a natural tendency to seek internal coherence. When this consistency is challenged there are essentially 2 ways in which this contradiction is resolved. We either change our behaviour to sustainably reduce the discomfort caused by disharmony or hold onto our beliefs that don’t reflect reality and try to justify the behaviour in some way. For example a smoker who cares about their health might justify the act of smoking by downplaying the negative effects and or convincing themselves of the cigarette’s benefits (e.g. stress relief). Another example would be someone who is against animal abuse and needless suffering, but purchases animal products. The difference between this and the cigarette example is that you are directly responsible for someone else's suffering and death.

    When you go into the supermarket and purchase for example a pack of cheese that is neatly wrapped and might even have a smiling cow on it you are being manipulated, because that is not the reality of the situation. This is nothing more than an attempt to keep people tractable and compliant, they are exploiting your cognitive dissonance („Ah I am glad I can consume meat, fish cheese, eggs and honey, what we do to them is fair after all“). The people behind this industry know the demand is shrinking and they are trying their very best to manipulate you into continuing to purchase their products. And even if they think what they are doing is right, what is it if not exploitation?
